Brand Story

The origin, the metaphor, the soul of the product
The Name

Tuned ‚Äî as in tuned in, fine-tuned, stay tuned. Your ADHD brain is an antenna that picks up every signal at once ‚Äî every sound, every idea, every worry, every brilliant tangent. The problem was never the antenna. The problem is that nobody gave you a dial. Tuned is the dial. It doesn't quiet your brain. It helps you find the right frequency.

The Garden

Your life is a beautiful garden that's been overgrown with thickets ‚Äî tasks tangling into each other, deadlines hiding behind weeds. Tuned cuts through the overgrowth so the flowers can grow. It doesn't plant new things. It reveals what's already there.

The Balloon

You're a balloon on an infinite string and the wind keeps changing. Tuned is the gentle current that guides you where you need to go next ‚Äî without pulling you down, without letting you get too lost in the clouds. Direction without heaviness.

The Audience

Millennial adults (late 30s to early 40s) with ADHD ‚Äî working parents juggling careers, kids in sports and school, a partner, a household, and a brain that loses track of Patriots shirt day. Many were diagnosed later in life and carry years of "why can't I just get it together?" They're self-aware, humor-forward, and exhausted by apps that add more cognitive load than they remove. They don't need another to-do list. They need one calm, capable friend who remembers everything so they don't have to.

Palette Philosophy

Designed for millennial adults in their early 40s ‚Äî the generation raised on earthy tones, mid-century warmth, and Kinfolk magazine. Visual Identity

Illustration style, UI principles, and visual language
Illustration Style

Organic, hand-drawn line work with subtle texture ‚Äî Headspace meets Kinfolk. Soft, imperfect shapes that feel human. Botanical elements (growing things, leaves, gardens clearing) and atmospheric elements (soft radio waves, warm glows, light cutting through fog). People as warm, diverse silhouettes.

UI Design Principles
Generous whitespace
Breathing room reduces cognitive load. Every element earns its space.
Progressive disclosure
Show the minimum. Reveal on interaction. Title + Save is enough.
No red for overdue
Amber and orange escalation only. Red = shame trigger for ADHD brains.
Large touch targets
Medication buttons, checkboxes, swipe zones ‚Äî designed for distracted fingers.
Persistent context
Timer banner, awareness banners every morning. Tuned doesn't forget.
Variable reward
6 celebration types, randomized. 10% Super. The brain craves novelty.
Sonic Identity

Soft, warm tones ‚Äî wind chimes, not phone pings. Completion = gentle meditation bell ding. Persistent reminders = slightly brighter but never jarring. Timer completion = a satisfied exhale. Avoid harsh buzzes, aggressive alarms, anything that triggers anxiety. The sound palette matches the visual palette: natural, warm, human.
