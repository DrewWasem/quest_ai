üõí Prompt Quest: Pre-Built Asset Shopping List

Every image, animation, and sound you need ‚Äî mapped to free sources. What exists vs. what you'll create.

üéØ The Goal Phaser assembles, never generates. Every actor, prop, backdrop, and reaction must be pre-loaded before the game starts. This document is your Day 1 download checklist ‚Äî grab everything that already exists for free, identify the gaps, and plan how to fill them.

üì¶ Source Libraries Overview

Kenney.nl CC0

60,000+ assets. Clean, consistent style. Perfect for props, backgrounds, UI, and audio. The foundation of your asset library.

itch.io Mixed Licenses

Thousands of free packs. Best for animated character sprites with multiple animation states. Check license per pack (filter CC0).

LottieFiles Free Tier

Thousands of free JSON animations. Perfect for reactions (confetti, explosions, hearts, stars). Lightweight overlay system.

OpenGameArt.org CC0/CC-BY

Community-contributed sprites. Good for filling gaps. Tiny Creatures pack expands Kenney's style with monsters/animals.

CraftPix.net Free Section

High-quality animated monster & creature packs. Free section has octopus, dinosaur, and robot sprites with animation states.

Opus 4.6 SVG Gen AI Generated

Fill gaps by generating SVG sprites with Claude. Convert to PNG sprite sheets. Stretch goal for truly custom assets.

üé≠ ACTORS ‚Äî Animated Character Sprites

Each actor needs a sprite atlas (JSON + PNG) with named animation states. The more states, the more expressive the scene scripts can be.

‚ö†Ô∏è Gap: No pack has eat, dance, cry, sneeze-fire, wave These are custom "funny" animations unique to your game. You'll need to either: (1) Create simple frame-by-frame sprite edits from a base monster sprite, (2) Use Opus 4.6 to generate SVG frames, or (3) Simplify the vocabulary ‚Äî use "happy" instead of "dance", show text emotes instead of complex animations.

A skilled pixel artist can do this in 1-2 hours; alternatively, keep animations simple (idle + talk + happy + sad covers 80% of scenes).

üß† Critical Insight: Reduce the Vocabulary, Not the Comedy

The biggest realization from this audit: You don't need 9 animation states per character. You need 3-4 core states + Phaser effects that SIMULATE the rest. The comedy comes from the scene script narration and the unexpected combinations, not from pixel-perfect custom animations.

Simplified Actor Vocabulary (What You Actually Need to Download)

Animation StateHow It's UsedExists in Free Packs?
idleDefault standing pose‚úÖ Every sprite pack has this
walk / runMoving across screen‚úÖ Most packs have this
happy / celebrateSuccess state‚ö†Ô∏è Some packs ‚Äî or use idle + bounce tween + confetti Lottie
sad / hurtFail state‚úÖ Most combat packs have "hurt" animation
action / attackDoing something (casting, eating, playing)‚úÖ "Attack" animation in combat packs works

Everything else is a Phaser effect on top of these 5 base states:

"Fancy" State in ScriptActually Implemented As

blushidle sprite + red tint overlay (Phaser setTint)

‚úÖ This means: You only need to download sprites with idle + walk + action + hurt states. The comedy animations are ALL created by combining those base states with Phaser tweens + Lottie overlays + sound effects + prop positioning. This is a massive simplification that turns your Day 1 from "source 50+ animation states" to "download 6 sprite packs + 14 Lotties + 5 audio packs."
